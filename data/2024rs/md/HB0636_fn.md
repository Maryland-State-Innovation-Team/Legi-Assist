Allegany County - Property Tax Credit - Evergreen Heritage Center 
 
   
This bill authorizes Allegany County and municipalities in the county to grant a property 
tax credit for property that is owned by Evergreen Heritage Center Foundation or 
Evergreen Heritage Center Holdings. The bill takes effect June 1, 2024, and applies to 
taxable years beginning after June 30, 2024. 
   
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. 
  
Local Effect:  Allegany County property tax revenues may decrease by approximately 
$5,300 annually beginning in FY 2025 to the extent the property tax credit is granted. 
County expenditures are not affected.    
  
Small Business Effect:  Potential meaningful. Evergreen Heritage Center Holdings could 
realize a reduction in county property taxes.

Local Fiscal Effect:  Evergreen Heritage Center Holdings owns one property located in 
Mt. Savage, Maryland. The property has a fiscal 2025 assessment of $546,300. As a result 
of the property tax credit under the bill, Allegany County property tax revenues may 
decrease by approximately $5,300 annually beginning in fiscal 2025. This estimate is based 
on a county property tax rate of $0.9750 per $100 of assessment.  
 
The Evergreen Heritage Center, located on 130 acres in Western Maryland, is a historic 
farm that pre-dates the Revolutionary War. The property has been on the Maryland 
Historical Trust inventory of properties since 1976 and was added to the National Register 
in 2015. The heritage center features a six-bedroom farmhouse and barn originally built 
more than 200 years ago, plus over 100 acres of managed forest, multiple gardens and 
orchards, and landscaped grounds.
