Libraries - Regional Resource Centers - Governing Board Membership 
 
 
This departmental bill increases the maximum authorized number of public members (from 
one to two) on the governing board for each regional library resource center 
(e.g., Eastern Shore, Southern Maryland, Western Maryland). The bill takes effect 
July 1, 2024. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. Altering the appointment process of board members does not affect 
governmental finances. 
  
Local Effect:  None. Altering the appointment process of board members does not affect 
governmental finances. 
 
Small Business Effect:  The Maryland State Library Agency (MSLA) has determined that 
this bill has minimal or no impact on small business (attached). The Department of 
Legislative Services concurs with this assessment. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law/Background:  Chapters 676 and 677 of 2023 altered the governance 
structure of the three public libraries designated as regional resource centers 
(e.g., Eastern Shore, Southern Maryland, Western Maryland) by establishing a governing 
board for each regional resource center. 
 
Specifically, for the Eastern Shore and Southern Maryland regional resource centers, the 
governing board consists of the following members:  (1) a library trustee from each

The initial members of the governing board must be appointed by the Regional Advisory 
Council; thereafter, the governing board must appoint future board members. 
 
For the Western Maryland regional resource center, the Board of Trustees of the 
Washington County Free Library serves as the governing board. In addition, the bylaws of 
the Washington County Free Library govern the composition, appointment, qualifications, 
terms, and structure of the governing board. 
 
According to MSLA, increasing the maximum number of public members will improve 
the diversity of the regional resource center boards and allow for more equitable 
representation of various communities served by the centers. MSLA further notes that, 
currently, board membership consists overwhelmingly of retirees, with minimal 
representation of other demographic groups. Under the bill, MSLA states that regional 
resource centers will have greater flexibility in recruiting board members that are aligned 
with library and community initiatives.
