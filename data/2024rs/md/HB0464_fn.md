Health Occupations – Practice Audiology – Definition 
 
 
This bill specifies that “practice audiology” means to (1) evaluate, diagnose, manage, and 
treat auditory or vestibular conditions in the human ear; (2) prescribe, order, sell dispense, 
or fit hearing aids to an individual for the correction or relief of a condition for which 
hearing aids are worn; (3) prescribe, order, sell, dispense, or externally fit a sound processor 
to an osseo-integrated device for the correction or relief of a condition for which 
osseo-integrated devices are worn; and (4) prescribe, order, sell dispense, or externally fit 
a “sound processor” (a nonsurgical, external unit that attaches to an internal 
osseo-integrated device or cochlear implant) to a cochlear implant for the correction or 
relief of a condition for which cochlear implants are worn. The bill also specifies what 
“practice audiology” includes and does not include.  
   
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  The State Board of Examiners for Audiologists, Hearing Aid Dispensers, 
Speech-Language Pathologists, and Music Therapists can update regulations with existing 
budgeted resources. Revenues are not affected.  
  
Local Effect:  None. 
  
Small Business Effect:  Minimal.      
  
 
Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  “Practice audiology” includes the (1) conducting of health screenings; 
(2) removal of a foreign body from the external auditory canal that is not impacted to the 
point it requires anesthesia; (3) removal of cerumen from the external auditory canal that

is not impacted to the point it requires anesthesia; (4) ordering of cultures and bloodwork 
testing as it relates to the auditory or vestibular conditions in the human ear; (5) ordering 
and performing of in-office nonradiographic scanning or imaging of the external auditory 
canal; and (6) ordering of radiographic imaging as it relates to the auditory or vestibular 
conditions in the human ear.  
 
“Practice audiology” does not include (1) surgery using an instrument, including a laser, 
scalpel, needle, cautery, a cryoprobe, or a suture, in which human tissue is cut, burned, 
vaporized, removed, or otherwise permanently altered by mechanical means, laser, 
ionizing radiation, ultrasound, or other means; (2) osseo-integrated device surgery; 
(3) cochlear implant surgery; or (4) the preparation, operation, or performance of 
radiographic imaging. 
 
Current Law:  Generally, an individual must be licensed by the State Board of Examiners 
for Audiologists, Speech-Language Pathologists, and Music Therapists to provide hearing 
aid services, practice audiology, practice speech-language pathology, or practice music 
therapy in Maryland.     
 
As updated by Chapter 60 and 61 of 2023, “practice audiology” means to (1) apply the 
principals, methods, and procedures of measurement, prediction, evaluation, testing, 
counseling, consultation, and instruction that relate to the development and disorders of 
hearing, vestibular functions, and related language and speech disorders to prevent or 
modify the disorders or assist individuals in hearing and auditory and related skills for 
communication and (2) prescribing, ordering, selling, dispensing, or fitting of a hearing aid 
to an individual for the correction or relief of a condition for which such devices are worn.
