Bond Sales - Notification of the Public 
 
 
This bill authorizes the Board of Public Works (BPW) and the State Treasurer to give 
public notice of a State bonds sale through the Municipal Market Monitor (TM3) or a 
similar service or method as the State Treasurer deems appropriate. The bill takes effect 
June 1, 2023. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  Potential minimal decrease in general fund expenditures as the bill will 
enable BPW and the State Treasurer to utilize less costly forms of notification. Revenues 
are not affected. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  BPW must give public notice of the sale of State bonds at least 10 days 
prior to the public sale. The notice must state the date, time, and place of the public sale. 
 
BPW must sell State bonds to the bidder who offers the lowest net interest cost to the State 
or meets the other terms or conditions of sale or issuance set by BPW. However, BPW may 
reject all bids if it determines that no bid is satisfactory and that it is in the best interest of 
the State to reject all bids.

By resolution, BPW may postpone the time for receipt of proposals for the bonds without

The State Treasurer must provide notice of the new date and time of sale at least 24 hours 
prior to the time proposals are to be submitted; however, the new date may not be more 
than 30 days after the originally scheduled date of sale. The public notice may be given by 
Munifacts News Service or a similar service or method the State Treasurer deems 
appropriate.
