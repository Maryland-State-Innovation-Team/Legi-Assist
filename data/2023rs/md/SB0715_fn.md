State Board of Foresters - Sunset Extension 
 
 
This departmental bill extends the termination date for the State Board of Foresters within 
the Maryland Department of Labor (MDL) by 10 years to July 1, 2035.   
   
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  General fund revenues and expenditures for the board are maintained from 
FY 2026 through 2035. 
  
Local Effect:  None.  
  
Small Business Effect:  MDL has determined that this bill has minimal or no impact on 
small business (attached). The Department of Legislative Services (DLS) concurs with this 
assessment. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law/Background:  The State Board of Foresters licenses and regulates foresters 
to ensure the appropriate use of natural and timber resources, watershed protection, and the 
preservation of ecological systems and wildlife habitats. It has the authority to investigate 
complaints against licensed foresters and deny, suspend, or revoke a license and reprimand 
a licensee for specified offenses. Instead of or in addition to suspending or revoking a 
license, the board may impose a civil penalty of up to $5,000 for each violation.  
 
The board is 1 of approximately 60 regulatory entities and activities subject to termination 
unless reauthorized periodically through statute. Under the Maryland Program Evaluation
