Judicial Proceedings

Corrections - Incarcerated Individuals 
 
 
This bill repeals the term “inmate” from statute and substitutes the term “incarcerated 
individual.” 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  None. The bill’s change is technical in nature and does not directly affect 
governmental finances or operations. 
  
Local Effect:  None. 
  
Small Business Effect:  None. 
  
 
Analysis 
 
Current Law:  The term “inmate” is used in the Correctional Services, Courts and Judicial 
Proceedings, Commercial Law, Criminal Law, Criminal Procedure, Education, 
Health-General, Labor and Employment, Public Safety, Public Utilities, State Government, 
and State Personnel and Pensions Articles to reference individuals confined within State 
and local correctional facilities. 
 
 
Additional Information 
 
Prior Introductions:  Similar legislation has not been introduced within the last 
three years.
