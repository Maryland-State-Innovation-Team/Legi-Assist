1 1. by a qualified health care provider;

2 2. from the body or clothing of a living or deceased person;

3 3. following an allegation or suspicion of sexual assault; and

4 4. for the purpose of preserving the physical evidence.

5 (ii) “Sexual assault evidence collection kit” does not include a
6 self–administered sexual assault evidence collection kit.

7 (e) A sexual assault evidence collection kit shall be submitted to a forensic
8 laboratory for analysis unless:

9 (1) there is clear evidence disproving the allegation of sexual assault;

10 (2) the facts alleged, if true, could not be interpreted to violate a provision
11 of Title 3, Subtitle 2, Title 3, Subtitle 3, Title 3, Subtitle 6, or Title 11, Subtitle 3 of the
12 Criminal Law Article;

13 (3) the victim from whom the evidence was collected declines to give
14 consent for analysis; or

15 (4) the suspect’s profile has been collected for entry as a convicted offender
16 for a qualifying offense in the Combined DNA Index System (CODIS) maintained by the
17 Federal Bureau of Investigation and the suspect has pleaded guilty to the offense that led
18 to the sexual assault evidence collection kit.

19 (g) Except as provided in subsection (e) of this section, an investigating law
20 enforcement agency that receives a sexual assault evidence collection kit shall:

21 (1) submit the kit and all requested associated reference standards to a
22 forensic laboratory for analysis within 30 days of receipt of the kit [and all requested
23 associated reference standards]; and

24 (2) make use of certified sexual assault crisis programs or other qualified
25 community–based sexual assault victim service organizations that can provide services and
26 support to survivors of sexual assault.

27 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
28 October 1, 2026.
