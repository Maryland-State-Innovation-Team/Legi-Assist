A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Veterans Benefits Matters – Claim Servicers – Requirements

3 FOR the purpose of requiring a person that is not V.A. accredited that is seeking
4 compensation for certain services related to veterans benefits matters to enter into
5 a certain written fee agreement with the recipient of the services that complies with
6 certain laws; requiring the fee agreement to contain a certain notice; requiring a
7 certain notice to be given orally and acknowledged in writing; prohibiting the
8 imposition or collection of a certain fee under certain circumstances; providing for
9 the enforcement of certain provisions of this Act; repealing certain advertising and
10 disclosure requirements for veterans benefits services and veterans benefits appeals
11 services; and generally relating to veterans benefits matters.

1 (iii) a radio, television, computer network or airwave, or electronic
2 transmission that solicits business for or promotes a person offering veterans benefits
3 appeals services.

4 (3) “Veterans benefits appeals services” means any services that a veteran
5 might reasonably require in order to appeal a denial of federal, state, or local veterans
6 benefits, including denials of disability, limited income, home loan, insurance, education
7 and training, health care, burial and memorial, and dependent and survivor benefits.

8 (4) “Veterans benefits services” means any services a veteran or a family
9 member of a veteran might reasonably use in order to obtain federal, state, or local veterans
10 benefits.

11 (b) (1) Before entering into an agreement with an individual for the provision
12 of veterans benefits services or veterans benefits appeals services, a person who charges a
13 fee for those services shall:

14 (i) provide a written disclosure statement to each individual; and

15 (ii) obtain the individual’s signature on the written disclosure
16 statement acknowledging that the individual understands the disclosure statement.

17 (2) The written disclosure statement shall:

18 (i) be on a form approved by the Secretary; and

19 (ii) state that veterans benefits services and veterans benefits
20 appeals services are offered at no cost by the Department and other veterans services
21 organizations accredited by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs.

22 (c) A person who charges a fee for providing veterans benefits appeals services
23 shall provide in any advertisement for appeals services notice that appeals services are also
24 offered at no cost by the Department and other veterans services organizations accredited
25 by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs.

26 (d) (1) A person who violates the provisions of this section is subject to a civil
27 penalty of not more than $1,000 for each violation.

28 (2) Civil penalties shall be in an amount ordered by the District Court in
29 an action brought by the Attorney General.

30 (3) Each day a violation continues is a separate violation.

31 (4) Any civil penalty collected shall be deposited in the Maryland Veterans
32 Trust Fund.]
