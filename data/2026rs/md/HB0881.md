A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Task Force on Not–for–Profit Funeral Establishments

3 FOR the purpose of establishing the Task Force on Not–for–Profit Funeral Establishments
4 to study the feasibility and necessity of the establishment of not–for–profit funeral
5 establishments in the State; and generally relating to the Task Force on
6 Not–for–Profit Funeral Establishments.

7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That:

9 (a) There is a Task Force on Not–for–Profit Funeral Establishments.

10 (b) The Task Force consists of the following members:

11 (1) one member of the Senate of Maryland, appointed by the President of
12 the Senate;

13 (2) one member of the House of Delegates, appointed by the Speaker of the
14 House;

15 (3) one representative from the Funeral Consumers Alliance of Maryland
16 and Environs, designated by the president of the Alliance;

17 (4) one representative from the Maryland State Funeral Directors
18 Association, designated by the president of the Association; and

19 (5) the following members, appointed by the Secretary of Health:

20 (i) one representative of a funeral establishment that serves
21 customers who are primarily African American;

1 (ii) one representative of a funeral establishment that serves
2 customers who are primarily Catholic;

3 (iii) one representative of a funeral establishment that serves
4 customers who are primarily Jewish;

5 (iv) one representative of a funeral establishment that serves
6 customers who are primarily Muslim; and

7 (v) one representative of a funeral establishment that serves
8 customers who are primarily Protestant.

9 (c) The chair of the Task Force shall be selected by a majority vote of the members
10 of the Task Force.

11 (d) The State Board of Morticians and Funeral Directors shall provide staff for
12 the Task Force.

13 (e) A member of the Task Force:

14 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Task Force; but

15 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
16 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.

17 (f) The Task Force shall analyze:

18 (1) the number of individuals licensed to practice funeral direction in the
19 State;

20 (2) the number of corporations that applied for a license to practice funeral
21 direction;

22 (3) the number of applications to practice funeral direction that were
23 denied because of the ineligibility of the applicant;

24 (4) the reason each application for a license to practice funeral direction
25 was denied;

26 (5) laws and regulations of other jurisdictions that authorize not–for–profit
27 entities to practice funeral direction;

28 (6) the potential impact of authorizing not–for–profit entities to practice
29 funeral direction in the State; and

30 (7) the feasibility and necessity of the establishment of not–for–profit
31 funeral establishments in the State.
