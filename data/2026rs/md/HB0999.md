A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Alcoholic Beverages – Class 9 Limited Distillery License – Alteration

3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the holder of a Class 9 limited distillery license to
4 manufacture, rectify, or bottle more than one brand of alcoholic beverage;
5 authorizing the holder to manufacture or rectify up to a certain amount of product
6 of an entity other than the holder; and generally relating to Class 9 limited distillery
7 licenses.

8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
9 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
10 Section 2–203
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

15 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis

16 2–203.

17 (a) There is a Class 9 limited distillery license.

18 (b) The limited distillery license may be issued only to a holder of a:

19 (1) Class D beer, wine, and liquor license where sales for both
20 on– and off–premises consumption are permitted for use on the premises for which the
21 Class D license was issued; or

1 (2) Class B beer, wine, and liquor license where sales for both
2 on– and off–premises consumption are permitted for use on the premises for which the
3 Class B license was issued.

4 (c) A holder of the limited distillery license:

5 (1) may establish and operate a plant in the State for distilling, rectifying,
6 and bottling brandy, rum, whiskey, alcohol, and neutral spirits [if the holder:

7 (i) maintains only one brand at any one time for each product of
8 brandy, rum, whiskey, alcohol, and neutral spirits that is distilled, rectified, and sold; and

9 (ii) does not manufacture or rectify product of any other brand for
10 another entity];

11 (2) may acquire bulk alcoholic beverages from the holder of a distillery or
12 rectifying license in the State or from the holder of a nonresident dealer’s permit;

13 (3) after acquiring an individual storage permit, may store on the licensed
14 premises those products manufactured under the license;

15 (4) except as authorized under subsection (d)(1) of this section, may sell
16 and deliver those products manufactured under the license only to a licensed wholesaler in
17 the State or person authorized to acquire distilled spirits in another state and not to a
18 county dispensary;

19 (5) may sell the products manufactured under the license at retail in a
20 manner consistent with the underlying Class D or Class B license;

21 (6) may conduct guided tours of that portion of the licensed premises used
22 for the limited distillery operation; and

23 (7) may serve not more than three samples of products manufactured at
24 the licensed premises, with each sample consisting of not more than one–half ounce from a
25 single product, to persons who:

26 (i) have attained the legal drinking age;

27 (ii) participated in a guided tour; and

28 (iii) are present on that portion of the premises used for the limited
29 distillery operation.

30 (d) A holder of the limited distillery license may not:

31 (1) apply for or possess a wholesaler’s license, except for a Class 8 liquor
32 wholesaler’s license;
