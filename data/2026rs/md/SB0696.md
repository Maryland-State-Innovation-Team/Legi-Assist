2 SENATE BILL 696

1 (c) The Commissioner is the head of the Office of Financial Regulation.

2 2–123.

3 (A) THE OFFICE OF FINANCIAL REGULATION SHALL:

4 (1) ESTABLISH AND MAINTAIN A DATABASE ON THE OFFICE’S
5 WEBSITE FOR COMPLAINTS FILED BY STATE RESIDENTS AGAINST FINANCIAL
6 INSTITUTIONS FOR UNFAIR, ABUSIVE, OR DECEPTIVE TRADE PRACTICES; AND

7 (2) PUBLISH A QUARTERLY ANALYSIS OF THE TRENDS IN AND
8 RESOLUTIONS TO THE COMPLAINTS LISTED ON THE OFFICE’S WEBSITE.

9 (B) (1) THE PURPOSE OF THE DATABASE IS TO PROVIDE TRANSPARENCY
10 TO CONSUMERS ABOUT PRODUCTS, ISSUES, OR COMPANIES ABOUT WHICH A
11 CONSUMER HAS FILED A COMPLAINT.

12 (2) EACH COMPLAINT SHALL BE IN NARRATIVE FORM WRITTEN BY
13 THE CONSUMER DESCRIBING THE CONSUMER’S EXPERIENCES THAT LED TO THE
14 FILING OF THE COMPLAINT.

15 (3) THE COMMISSIONER SHALL ENSURE THAT PERSONALLY
16 IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION IS REDACTED FROM THE NARRATIVE.

17 (C) THE DATABASE SHALL:

18 (1) BE REGULARLY UPDATED;

19 (2) BE SEARCHABLE BY METRICS DETERMINED BY THE
20 COMMISSIONER THAT WILL INCREASE THE TRANSPARENCY FOR CONSUMERS AND
21 THE OFFICE’S ABILITY TO ANALYZE THE COMPLAINTS; AND

22 (3) BE DOWNLOADABLE.

23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
24 October 1, 2026.
