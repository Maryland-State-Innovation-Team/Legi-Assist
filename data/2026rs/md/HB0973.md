A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Open Meetings Act – Enhanced Requirements for Educational Entities and
3 Retention Requirements

4 FOR the purpose of making certain enhanced Open Meetings Act requirements applicable
5 to certain educational entities; requiring certain entities subject to enhanced Open
6 Meetings Act requirements to retain certain meeting minutes and recordings for a
7 certain period of time; and generally relating to the Open Meetings Act and
8 educational entities.

1 (b) A public body specified in subsection (a) of this section shall make publicly
2 available on its website:

3 (1) each open meeting agenda, together with a summary of any finalized
4 documents, written testimony from the public, and other materials that the public body
5 will vote on at the open meeting:

6 (i) at least 48 hours in advance of each meeting; or

7 (ii) if the meeting is being held due to an emergency, a natural
8 disaster, or any other unanticipated situation, as far in advance of the meeting as
9 practicable;

10 (2) meeting minutes from the portions of a meeting that were held in open
11 session, not more than 2 business days after the minutes are approved; and

12 (3) subject to subsections (f) and (g) of this section, live video streaming of
13 each portion of a meeting that is held in open session.

14 (c) To the extent practicable, each open meeting agenda shall indicate:

15 (1) whether the public body intends to adjourn the open session to a closed
16 session; and

17 (2) if applicable, the expected time at which the public body intends to
18 adjourn the open session to a closed session.

19 (d) (1) The public body shall approve meeting minutes in a timely manner.

20 (2) Each open meeting agenda shall include consideration of the meeting
21 minutes from the most recent meeting.

22 (3) The requirement under paragraph (2) of this subsection does not apply
23 to the agenda of an emergency meeting of a public body if consideration of the meeting
24 minutes from the most recent meeting is not practicable because of the need to hold an
25 emergency meeting.

26 (e) The public body shall maintain on its website:

27 (1) meeting minutes made available under subsection (b) of this section for
28 a minimum of 5 years after the date of the meeting; and

29 (2) except as provided in subsections (f), (g), and (g–1) of this section, a
30 complete and unedited archived video recording of each open meeting for which live video
31 streaming was made available under subsection (b) of this section for a minimum of 1 year
32 after the date of the meeting.

1 (E–1) THE PUBLIC BODY SHALL KEEP A COPY OF THE MINUTES OF EACH
2 SESSION AND ANY RECORDING MADE UNDER SUBSECTION (E)(2) OF THIS SECTION
3 FOR AT LEAST 15 YEARS AFTER THE DATE OF THE SESSION.

4 (f) (1) This subsection applies only to the Maryland Stadium Authority.

5 (2) If the Maryland Stadium Authority meets by telephone conference, the
6 Authority shall:

7 (i) make publicly available on its website live audio streaming of
8 each portion of the meeting that is held in open session; and

9 (ii) maintain on its website a complete and unedited archived audio
10 recording of each meeting for which live audio streaming was made available for a
11 minimum of 1 year after the date of the meeting.

12 (g) (1) This subsection applies only to the Maryland Transportation Authority.

13 (2) The Maryland Transportation Authority is required to provide live
14 video streaming of a meeting only if the meeting is held at:

15 (i) the headquarters of the Maryland Transportation Authority; or

16 (ii) a location where the Maryland Transportation Authority held at
17 least 10 meetings during the immediately preceding calendar year.

18 (g–1) The Maryland Department of Transportation, with respect to quarterly public
19 meetings held by the Vision Zero coordinator on the implementation of Vision Zero under §
20 8–1004 of the Transportation Article, shall maintain on its website a complete and unedited
21 archived video recording of the meeting for a minimum of 5 years after the date of the
22 meeting.

23 (h) The Department of Information Technology shall provide the technical staff,
24 support, and equipment necessary to livestream the open meetings of:

25 (1) the Maryland Transportation Authority;

26 (2) the State Board of Elections; and

27 (3) the State Ethics Commission.

28 (i) For purposes of this title, a project site visit or educational field tour may not
29 be considered a meeting of a public body listed under subsection (a) of this section if no
30 organizational business is conducted.

31 (j) Nothing in this section may be construed to prevent a public body from
32 altering the agenda of a meeting after the agenda has been made available to the public.
