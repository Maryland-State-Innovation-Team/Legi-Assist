A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Criminal Law – Interference of Custody Orders – Penalties

3 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from knowingly and willfully interfering with a
4 certain custody order in a certain manner; and generally relating to custody orders.

5 BY adding to
6 Article – Criminal Law
7 Section 3–504
8 Annotated Code of Maryland
9 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

10 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
11 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

12 Article – Criminal Law

13 3–504.

14 (A) THIS SECTION APPLIES TO A CUSTODY ORDER OVER WHICH A COURT OF
15 THE STATE HAS JURISDICTION.

16 (B) A PERSON MAY NOT KNOWINGLY AND WILLFULLY INTERFERE WITH A
17 CUSTODY ORDER, BY ENTICING OR PERSUADING A MINOR AWAY OR WITHHOLDING A
18 MINOR FROM:

19 (1) THE MINOR’S LAWFUL CUSTODIAN;

20 (2) A COURT–APPOINTED GUARDIAN; OR

1 (3) A PERSON LAWFULLY STANDING IN LOCO PARENTIS.

2 (C) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION:

3 (1) FOR A FIRST OFFENSE, SHALL RECEIVE A WRITTEN WARNING;

10 (5) FOR A FIFTH OR SUBSEQUENT OFFENSE, IS GUILTY OF A
11 MISDEMEANOR AND ON CONVICTION IS SUBJECT TO IMPRISONMENT NOT
12 EXCEEDING 1 YEAR.

13 (D) A WRITTEN WARNING FOR A VIOLATION ISSUED UNDER SUBSECTION
14 (C)(1) OF THIS SECTION:

15 (1) SHALL BE SIGNED BY THE LAW ENFORCEMENT OFFICER ISSUING

17 (2) SHALL CONTAIN:

18 (I) THE NAME, ADDRESS, AND DATE OF BIRTH OF THE PERSON
19 IN VIOLATION OF THIS SECTION;

20 (II) THE DATE AND TIME THAT THE VIOLATION OCCURRED;

21 (III) A DESCRIPTION OF THE VIOLATION THAT OCCURRED; AND

22 (IV) NOTICE OF THE POTENTIAL PENALTIES THAT MAY BE
23 IMPOSED FOR SUBSEQUENT VIOLATIONS.
