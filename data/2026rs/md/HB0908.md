A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Civil Actions – Failure to Restrict Distribution of Sexual Material Harmful to
3 Minors

4 FOR the purpose of establishing that a commercial entity that distributes certain sexual
5 material harmful to minors on the Internet and that fails to perform certain age
6 verification methods is liable for certain damages under certain circumstances;
7 prohibiting a commercial entity or third party that performs a certain age
8 verification of an individual from retaining any identifying information of an
9 individual under certain circumstances; and generally relating to the distribution of
10 sexual material harmful to minors.

11 BY adding to
12 Article – Courts and Judicial Proceedings
13 Section 3–2701 and 3–2702 to be under the new subtitle “Subtitle 27. Failure to
14 Restrict Distribution of Sexual Material Harmful to Minors”

2 (I) “SEXUAL MATERIAL HARMFUL TO MINORS” MEANS MATERIAL THAT:

3 (1) IS OBSCENE; AND

4 (2) EXPLOITS, IS DEVOTED TO, OR PRINCIPALLY CONSISTS OF
5 DESCRIPTIONS OF ACTUAL, SIMULATED, OR ANIMATED DISPLAYS OR DEPICTIONS
6 OF:

7 (I) AN INDIVIDUAL’S PUBIC HAIR, ANUS, GENITALS, OR THE
8 NIPPLES OF THE FEMALE BREAST;

9 (II) TOUCHING, CARESSING, OR FONDLING OF THE NIPPLES,
10 BREASTS, BUTTOCKS, ANUSES, OR GENITALS; OR

11 (III) SEXUAL INTERCOURSE, MASTURBATION, SODOMY,
12 BESTIALITY, ORAL COPULATION, FLAGELLATION, EXCRETORY FUNCTIONS,
13 EXHIBITIONS, OR ANY OTHER SEXUAL ACT.

14 (J) “SUBSTANTIAL PORTION” MEANS MORE THAN ONE–THIRD OF THE
15 TOTAL MATERIAL ON A WEBSITE.

16 (K) (1) “TRANSACTIONAL DATA” MEANS A SEQUENCE OF INFORMATION
17 THAT DOCUMENTS AN EXCHANGE, AN AGREEMENT, OR A TRANSFER BETWEEN AN
18 INDIVIDUAL, A COMMERCIAL ENTITY, OR A THIRD PARTY USED FOR THE PURPOSE
19 OF SATISFYING A REQUEST OR AN EVENT.

20 (2) “TRANSACTIONAL DATA” INCLUDES RECORDS FROM MORTGAGE,
21 EDUCATION, AND EMPLOYMENT ENTITIES.

1 (B) THIS SECTION DOES NOT APPLY TO ANY BONA FIDE NEWS OR PUBLIC
2 INTEREST BROADCAST, WEBSITE VIDEO, REPORT, OR EVENT AND MAY NOT BE
3 CONSTRUED TO AFFECT THE RIGHTS OF ANY NEWS–GATHERING ORGANIZATION
4 EMPLOYEES.

5 (C) (1) A COMMERCIAL ENTITY THAT KNOWINGLY AND INTENTIONALLY
6 DISTRIBUTES SEXUAL MATERIAL HARMFUL TO MINORS ON THE INTERNET FROM A
7 WEBSITE THAT CONTAINS A SUBSTANTIAL PORTION OF SEXUAL MATERIAL HARMFUL
8 TO MINORS SHALL PERFORM REASONABLE AGE VERIFICATION METHODS TO VERIFY
9 THE AGE OF INDIVIDUALS ATTEMPTING TO ACCESS THE MATERIAL.

10 (2) A COMMERCIAL ENTITY OR THIRD PARTY THAT PERFORMS AN
11 AGE VERIFICATION REQUIRED UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY NOT RETAIN ANY
12 IDENTIFYING INFORMATION ABOUT INDIVIDUALS ATTEMPTING TO ACCESS SEXUAL
13 MATERIAL HARMFUL TO MINORS AFTER ACCESS TO THE MATERIAL HAS BEEN
14 GRANTED OR DENIED.

15 (D) (1) A PARENT OR LEGAL GUARDIAN OF A MINOR MAY BRING AN
16 ACTION AGAINST A COMMERCIAL ENTITY THAT VIOLATES SUBSECTION (C)(1) OF
17 THIS SECTION FOR DAMAGES RESULTING FROM THE MINOR ACCESSING SEXUAL
18 MATERIAL HARMFUL TO MINORS.

19 (2) A PERSON MAY BRING AN ACTION AGAINST A COMMERCIAL
20 ENTITY THAT VIOLATES SUBSECTION (C)(2) OF THIS SECTION FOR DAMAGES
21 RESULTING FROM THE RETENTION OF IDENTIFYING INFORMATION OF AN
22 INDIVIDUAL.

23 (3) A COURT MAY AWARD COSTS, REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES,
24 AND ANY OTHER RELIEF THE COURT CONSIDERS APPROPRIATE TO A PREVAILING
25 PLAINTIFF UNDER THIS SECTION.

26 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
27 October 1, 2026.
