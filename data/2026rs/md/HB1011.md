A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Workgroup on Supporting Transgender and Gender Diverse Students –
3 Established

4 FOR the purpose of establishing the Workgroup on Supporting Transgender and Gender
5 Diverse Students; and generally relating to the Workgroup on Supporting
6 Transgender and Gender Diverse Students.

7 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
8 That:

9 (a) There is a Workgroup on Supporting Transgender and Gender Diverse
10 Students.

11 (b) The Workgroup consists of the following members:

12 (1) two members of the Senate of Maryland, one member who lives in a
13 rural jurisdiction and one member who lives in an urban jurisdiction, appointed by the
14 President of the Senate;

15 (2) two members of the House of Delegates, one member who lives in a
16 rural jurisdiction and one member who lives in an urban jurisdiction, appointed by the
17 Speaker of the House;

18 (3) one representative of the State Department of Education, appointed by
19 the State Superintendent of Schools;

20 (4) one representative of the Maryland Association of Boards of Education,
21 appointed by the Executive Director of the Association;

22 (5) one representative of the Public School Superintendents’ Association of
23 Maryland, appointed by the Executive Director of the Association;

13 (12) two members of the Executive Branch of State government, appointed
14 by the Governor.

15 (c) One of the members appointed from the Senate of Maryland and one of the
16 members appointed from the House of Delegates shall serve as cochairs of the Workgroup.

17 (d) The State Department of Education shall provide staff for the Workgroup.

18 (e) A member of the Workgroup:

19 (1) may not receive compensation as a member of the Workgroup; but

20 (2) is entitled to reimbursement for expenses under the Standard State
21 Travel Regulations, as provided in the State budget.

22 (f) The Workgroup shall:

23 (1) study ways to support transgender and gender diverse students in
24 public schools in the State; and

25 (2) make recommendations regarding statewide statutory and regulatory
26 policies.

27 (g) On or before December 1, 2026, the Workgroup shall report its findings and
28 recommendations to the State Department of Education, the Governor, and, in accordance
29 with § 2–1257 of the State Government Article, the General Assembly.
