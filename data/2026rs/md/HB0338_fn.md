Department of General Services - Maryland Capitol Police - Statewide 
Jurisdiction 
 
 
This bill alters and expands the jurisdiction of the Department of General Services (DGS) 
and the Maryland Capital Police (MCP) within DGS. The bill takes effect July 1, 2026. 
 
 
Fiscal Summary 
 
State Effect:  DGS did not respond to multiple requests for information on the fiscal 
and operational impact of the bill. However, altering and expanding the jurisdiction of 
DGS and MCP is not anticipated to materially affect State finances. 
  
Local Effect:  The bill’s changes are not anticipated to materially affect local government 
operations or finances. 
 
Small Business Effect:  None. 
 
 
Analysis 
 
Bill Summary:  The bill repeals DGS’s jurisdiction over, and full police authority for, the 
enforcement of the criminal laws and the parking and motor vehicle laws as to the 
operation, maintenance, and protection of: 
 
 
buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 1984, were administered by the Office of 
Annapolis Public Buildings and Grounds and extending to the surrounding area that 
encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and 
grounds;

Instead, the bill establishes that DGS has statewide jurisdiction over and full police

buildings and grounds and extending to the surrounding area that encompasses 2,000 feet 
in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and (2) other public improvements 
or grounds designated by law or by BPW. 
 
In addition to these powers, an MCP police officer may investigate and otherwise  
enforce statewide the criminal laws, Title 12 of the Criminal Procedure Article  
(Forfeiture – Controlled Dangerous Substances Violations), and parking and motor vehicle 
laws. If an MCP police officer exercises these powers, notification of the investigation and 
enforcement must be provided as soon as reasonably practicable and as applicable to: 
 
 
the chief of police of a law enforcement agency, as specified, or the designee of the 
chief of police; 
 
in a county in which the Department of State Police (DSP) provides general law 
enforcement, (1) the commander of the local barracks for DSP or the designee of 
the commander and (2) the Secretary of State Police or the designee of the Secretary; 
 
in Baltimore City, (1) the Police Commissioner or the designee of the Police 
Commissioner and (2) the Sheriff or the designee of the Sheriff; and 
 
the Secretary of State Police or the designee of the Secretary, the Chief of Police of 
the Maryland Transit Administration Police Force or the designee of the Chief, the 
Chief of Police of the Maryland Department of Health Secured Transport and 
Investigation Unit or the designee of the Chief, and the Superintendent of the 
Natural Resources Police or the designee of the Superintendent.  
 
The required notification must be made in accordance with adopted State regulations, 
policies, procedures, and memoranda of understanding.

Current Law:  In accordance with the provisions of the State Personnel and Pensions 
Article, the Secretary of General Services may establish a police and security force, known 
as MCP of DGS, to protect people and property on or about improvements, grounds, and 
multiservice centers under the jurisdiction of the department, and in the surrounding areas 
of the buildings and grounds in the State, as specified. 
 
DGS has jurisdiction over the operation, maintenance, and protection of specified State 
buildings and public grounds in Annapolis and Baltimore. MCP is a full-service police 
department that provides law enforcement and security services for State buildings, State 
parking lots, and State parking garages in Annapolis and Baltimore. 
 
Specifically, DGS has jurisdiction over, and full police authority for, the enforcement of 
the criminal laws and the parking and motor vehicle laws as to the operation, maintenance, 
and protection of: 
 
 
buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 1984, were administered by the Office of 
Annapolis Public Buildings and Grounds and extending to the surrounding area that 
encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and 
grounds; 
 
buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 1984, were administered by the Office of 
Baltimore Public Buildings and Grounds and extending to the surrounding area that 
encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those buildings and

 
multiservice centers designated by law or by BPW and extending to the surrounding 
areas that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those 
multiservice centers;  
 
buildings and grounds that, on June 30, 2019, were administered by MD Labor, and 
extending to the surrounding area that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from 
the boundary of those buildings; 
 
State-owned or State-leased buildings and grounds and extending to the surrounding 
area that encompasses 1,000 feet in any direction from the boundary of those 
buildings and grounds; and 
 
other public improvements or grounds designated by law or by BPW.

Within the jurisdiction of MCP, an MCP police officer has all of the powers granted to a 
peace officer and a police officer of the State, provided the officer meets the legal 
requirements set forth by the Maryland Police Training and Standards Commission and is 
designated by DGS as a police officer. 
 
Each police employee, including an individual who is appointed to MCP for training before 
regular assignment as a police employee, must remain in a probationary status for a period 
of one year after the date of appointment to MCP. The Secretary may terminate the 
employment of a police employee in probationary status for any reason that the Secretary 
considers sufficient.
