A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Health Occupations – Human Trafficking Awareness Training

3 FOR the purpose of requiring each health occupations board to adopt certain regulations
4 and conduct certain outreach related to continuing education credits for a certain
5 human trafficking awareness training program; establishing requirements for the
6 training program; and generally relating to human trafficking awareness training
7 for health care practitioners.

8 BY adding to
9 Article – Health Occupations
10 Section 1–225.1
11 Annotated Code of Maryland
12 (2021 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

13 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
14 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

15 Article – Health Occupations

16 1–225.1.

17 (A) (1) IN THIS SECTION, “LOCAL UNIVERSITY” MEANS A PUBLIC OR
18 PRIVATE INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION IN THE STATE WITH A PUBLIC
19 HEALTH, LAW, OR SOCIAL WORK PROGRAM.

20 (2) “LOCAL UNIVERSITY” INCLUDES A PROGRAM WITHIN AN
21 INSTITUTION OF HIGHER EDUCATION DESCRIBED IN PARAGRAPH (1) OF THIS
22 SUBSECTION, SUCH AS THE UNIVERSITY OF MARYLAND SAFE CENTER FOR HUMAN
23 TRAFFICKING SURVIVORS.

1 (B) EACH HEALTH OCCUPATIONS BOARD SHALL:

2 (1) ADOPT REGULATIONS THAT ALLOW AN APPLICANT SEEKING
3 LICENSE OR CERTIFICATE RENEWAL TO RECEIVE CONTINUING EDUCATION CREDITS
4 FOR COMPLETING A HUMAN TRAFFICKING AWARENESS TRAINING PROGRAM
5 DESCRIBED IN SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION; AND

6 (2) CONDUCT OUTREACH TO MAKE INDIVIDUALS REGULATED BY THE
7 BOARD AWARE OF HUMAN TRAFFICKING AWARENESS TRAINING OPTIONS,
8 INCLUDING BY PROVIDING INFORMATION IN GENERAL BOARD COMMUNICATIONS
9 AND ON THE BOARD’S WEBSITE.

10 (C) A TRAINING PROGRAM FOR WHICH AN APPLICANT MAY RECEIVE
11 CONTINUING EDUCATION CREDITS UNDER THIS SECTION SHALL:

12 (1) BE APPROVED BY THE DEPARTMENT IN COLLABORATION WITH A
13 LOCAL UNIVERSITY AND IN CONSULTATION WITH THE MARYLAND HUMAN
14 TRAFFICKING TASK FORCE AND ORGANIZATIONS THAT PROVIDE DIRECT SERVICES
15 TO HUMAN TRAFFICKING SURVIVORS;

16 (2) BE AT LEAST 1 HOUR IN DURATION;

17 (3) BE READILY ACCESSIBLE AND PROVIDED TO AN INDIVIDUAL
18 DESCRIBED UNDER SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION FREE OF CHARGE; AND

19 (4) INCLUDE INSTRUCTION ON:

20 (I) THE DEFINITIONS AND FORMS OF SEX AND LABOR
21 TRAFFICKING UNDER FEDERAL AND STATE LAW;

22 (II) AN UNDERSTANDING OF THE TRAUMA–INFORMED CARE
23 MODEL;

24 (III) THE PHYSICAL, BEHAVIORAL, AND EMOTIONAL
25 INDICATORS OF HUMAN TRAFFICKING, PARTICULARLY THOSE PRESENTING IN A
26 HEALTH CARE SETTING;

27 (IV) APPROPRIATE SCREENING PROTOCOLS AND
28 DOCUMENTATION METHODS;

29 (V) MANDATORY REPORTING REQUIREMENTS UNDER STATE
30 LAW AND ESTABLISHED PROTOCOLS FOR REPORTING SUSPECTED CASES OF HUMAN
