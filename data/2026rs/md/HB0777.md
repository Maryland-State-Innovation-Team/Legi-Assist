A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Motor Vehicles – Required Equipment – Rear Occupant Alert Systems
3 (Hot Cars Act)

4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting a person from selling or offering for sale certain motor
5 vehicles manufactured or assembled after a certain date unless the motor vehicle is
6 equipped with a rear occupant alert system; prohibiting the Motor Vehicle
7 Administration from registering a motor vehicle sold in violation of this Act; and
8 generally relating to motor vehicles and rear occupant alert systems.

9 BY adding to
10 Article – Transportation
11 Section 22–422
12 Annotated Code of Maryland
13 (2020 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

14 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
15 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

16 Article – Transportation

17 22–422.

18 (A) IN THIS SECTION, “REAR OCCUPANT ALERT SYSTEM” MEANS A SYSTEM
19 THAT ALERTS THE OPERATOR OF A MOTOR VEHICLE TO INSPECT THE
20 REAR–DESIGNATED SEATING POSITIONS OF THE MOTOR VEHICLE AFTER THE
21 OPERATOR DEACTIVATES THE VEHICLE’S ENGINE OR MOTOR.

22 (B) THIS SECTION APPLIES ONLY TO A MOTOR VEHICLE WITH
23 REAR–DESIGNATED SEATING POSITIONS THAT IS:

1 (1) MANUFACTURED OR ASSEMBLED AT LEAST 1 YEAR AFTER THE
2 DATE ON WHICH THE NATIONAL HIGHWAY TRAFFIC SAFETY ADMINISTRATION
3 PROMULGATES FINAL REGULATIONS ESTABLISHING STANDARDS FOR REAR

7 (I) A CLASS A (PASSENGER) VEHICLE;

8 (II) A CLASS E (TRUCK) VEHICLE; OR

9 (III) A CLASS M (MULTIPURPOSE) VEHICLE.

10 (C) (1) A PERSON MAY NOT SELL OR OFFER FOR SALE IN THE STATE A
11 MOTOR VEHICLE THAT IS TO BE REGISTERED IN THE STATE UNLESS THE MOTOR
12 VEHICLE IS EQUIPPED WITH A REAR OCCUPANT ALERT SYSTEM THAT MEETS THE
13 REQUIREMENTS SET BY THE NATIONAL HIGHWAY TRAFFIC SAFETY
14 ADMINISTRATION.

15 (2) THE ADMINISTRATION MAY NOT REGISTER A MOTOR VEHICLE
16 SOLD IN VIOLATION OF THIS SUBSECTION.

17 (D) THE ADMINISTRATION SHALL ADOPT REGULATIONS NECESSARY TO
18 CARRY OUT THE PROVISIONS OF THIS SECTION, INCLUDING PROCEDURES FOR
19 DOCUMENTATION OF COMPLIANCE AT THE TIME OF SALE OR REGISTRATION.

20 (E) THE ADMINISTRATION MAY NOT ENFORCE THIS SECTION IF A FEDERAL
21 AGENCY DETERMINES IN WRITING THAT ENFORCEMENT OF THIS SECTION WOULD
22 CAUSE THE STATE TO BE INELIGIBLE TO RECEIVE ANY FEDERAL FUNDS.

23 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
24 October 1, 2029.
