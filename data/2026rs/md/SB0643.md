A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Natural Resources – Hunting – Management

3 FOR the purpose of providing that the Department of Natural Resources may authorize the
4 reduction of the deer population on certain property with deer damage using certain
5 methods; altering the time restrictions for Sunday deer hunting in certain counties
6 during deer firearms season; repealing the prohibition against hunting migratory
7 game birds on Sundays and authorizing the Department to allow Sunday hunting
8 for migratory game birds, subject to a certain requirement; repealing certain
9 provisions of law governing Sunday hunting on a county–by–county basis and
10 authorizing the Department to adopt regulations allowing Sunday hunting during
11 game bird and game mammal seasons, subject to a certain standard; and generally
12 relating to hunting management.

13 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,
14 Article – Natural Resources
15 Section 10–206 and 10–410(a)

1 10–206.

2 (A) The Department may reduce the wildlife population in any county, election
3 district, or other identifiable area of the State after a thorough investigation reveals that
4 protected wildlife is seriously injurious to agricultural or other interests in the affected
5 area. The method of reducing the wildlife population is at the Department’s discretion,
6 except that trapping is preferred whenever feasible. The Department shall dispose of
7 wildlife taken pursuant to the provisions of this section as the Department deems
8 advisable.

9 (B) (1) IN THIS SUBSECTION, “DEER DAMAGE” INCLUDES:

10 (I) WIDESPREAD DAMAGE CAUSED BY DEER, INCLUDING THE
11 ELIMINATION OF FOREST UNDERSTORY, TREE REGENERATION, AND NATIVE
12 PLANTS; AND

13 (II) THE DESTRUCTION OF AGRICULTURAL CROPS OR
14 LANDSCAPING, INCLUDING ORNAMENTAL LANDSCAPING, CAUSED BY DEER.

15 (2) THE DEPARTMENT MAY AUTHORIZE THE REDUCTION OF THE
16 DEER POPULATION ON ANY SINGLE, CONTIGUOUS PROPERTY OF AT LEAST 10 ACRES
17 WHERE THE DEPARTMENT HAS DOCUMENTED DEER DAMAGE.

18 (3) (I) THE METHOD OF REDUCING THE DEER POPULATION:

19 1. IS AT THE DEPARTMENT’S DISCRETION; AND

20 2. SUBJECT TO SUBPARAGRAPH (II) OF THIS
21 PARAGRAPH, MAY INCLUDE THE ISSUANCE OF A PERMIT TO A PROPERTY OWNER, OR
22 THE PROPERTY OWNER’S EMPLOYEE, LESSEE, OR AGENT, TO SHOOT DEER ON THE
23 PROPERTY ON SUNDAYS.

24 (II) A PERMIT ISSUED UNDER SUBPARAGRAPH (I)2 OF THIS
25 PARAGRAPH SHALL PROHIBIT DEER HUNTING ON SUNDAYS DURING DEER
26 FIREARMS SEASON WITHIN 25 YARDS OF A DEPARTMENT TRAIL DESIGNATED FOR
27 EQUESTRIAN USE.

28 (4) THE DEPARTMENT SHALL:

29 (I) MAP AREAS OF THE STATE TO WHICH A PERMIT MAY APPLY
30 UNDER THIS SUBSECTION; AND

31 (II) MAKE THE MAPS READILY AVAILABLE TO THE PUBLIC.
