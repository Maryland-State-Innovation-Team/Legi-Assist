A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Public Health – Cosmetic Products – Enforcement and Penalties for Prohibited
3 Ingredients
4 (Crown and Care Act – Protecting Communities From Harmful Hair Chemicals)

5 FOR the purpose of providing that a person who violates the prohibition on the
6 manufacture, sale, delivery, holding, or offering for sale a cosmetic product that
7 contains certain ingredients is liable for certain civil damages; authorizing the
8 Maryland Department of Health to investigate a complaint alleging that a person
9 included certain prohibited ingredients in cosmetic products; and generally relating
10 to prohibited ingredients in cosmetic products.

1 (e) (1) “Cosmetic” means any substance, or any component of a substance, that

1 (D) (1) IN ADDITION TO ANY OTHER PENALTIES UNDER THIS TITLE, A
2 PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS LIABLE FOR CIVIL DAMAGES SUSTAINED
3 BY THE INDIVIDUAL RESULTING FROM THE VIOLATION.

4 (2) IN ANY ACTION BROUGHT TO ENFORCE THIS SECTION, A COURT
5 MAY AWARD REASONABLE ATTORNEY’S FEES TO A PREVAILING PLAINTIFF.

6 (E) (1) THE DEPARTMENT MAY INVESTIGATE ANY COMPLAINT ALLEGING
7 THAT A PERSON HAS VIOLATED THIS SECTION.

8 (2) ON RECEIPT OF A COMPLAINT, A REPRESENTATIVE OF THE
9 DEPARTMENT, AT A REASONABLE TIME, MAY ENTER AND INSPECT THE PREMISES OF
10 THE PERSON LOCATED IN THE STATE TO DETERMINE COMPLIANCE WITH THIS
11 SECTION.

12 (3) THE PERSON MAY NOT:

13 (I) REFUSE TO GRANT ACCESS TO A REPRESENTATIVE WHO
14 REQUESTS TO ENTER AND INSPECT THE PREMISES OF THE PERSON UNDER
15 PARAGRAPH (2) OF THIS SUBSECTION; OR

16 (II) INTERFERE WITH AN INSPECTION UNDER PARAGRAPH (2)
17 OF THIS SUBSECTION.

18 (4) AN INVESTIGATION OF A PREMISES OF A PERSON CONDUCTED
19 UNDER THIS SUBSECTION MAY INCLUDE SAMPLING OF A COSMETIC PRODUCT TO
20 DETERMINE IF THE COSMETIC PRODUCT CONTAINS A TECHNICALLY UNAVOIDABLE
21 TRACE QUANTITY OF AN INGREDIENT LISTED IN SUBSECTION (B) OF THIS SECTION.

22 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect July
23 1, 2026.
