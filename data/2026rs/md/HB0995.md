A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Health Occupations – Behavioral Health Care Providers – Use of Artificial
3 Intelligence

4 FOR the purpose of prohibiting behavioral health care providers from using artificial
5 intelligence to provide behavioral health care to a patient; authorizing a behavioral
6 health care provider to use a system that employs artificial intelligence in
7 performing administrative support tasks under certain circumstances; requiring a
8 behavioral health care provider to obtain certain informed consent from patients if
9 the provider uses a certain system that employs artificial intelligence under certain
10 circumstances; and generally relating to the use of artificial intelligence by
11 behavioral health care providers.

4 (B) EXCEPT AS PROVIDED IN SUBSECTION (C) OF THIS SECTION, A
5 BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER MAY NOT USE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO
6 PROVIDE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE TO A PATIENT, INCLUDING FOR:

7 (1) ASSESSMENT, DIAGNOSIS, TREATMENT, OR COUNSELING;

8 (2) DEVELOPMENT OF A TREATMENT PLAN;

9 (3) CASE MANAGEMENT; OR

10 (4) THERAPEUTIC COMMUNICATIONS.

11 (C) (1) A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER MAY USE A SYSTEM THAT
12 EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO ASSIST THE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE
13 PROVIDER IN PERFORMING ADMINISTRATIVE SUPPORT TASKS IF THE BEHAVIORAL
14 HEALTH CARE PROVIDER:

15 (I) ENSURES COMPLIANCE WITH ALL APPLICABLE FEDERAL
16 AND STATE LAWS AND REGULATIONS GOVERNING PATIENT CONFIDENTIALITY AND
17 THE SECURITY OF HEALTH RECORDS, HEALTH–RELATED INFORMATION, AND OTHER
18 RELATED DATA;

19 (II) SIGNS AN AGREEMENT WITH THE PERSON THAT OWNS THE
20 SYSTEM THAT EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO ENSURE THAT:

21 1. PROTECTED HEALTH INFORMATION WILL BE KEPT
22 CONFIDENTIAL; AND

23 2. INFORMATION ACCESSED THROUGH THE USE OF THE
24 ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE MAY NOT BE USED TO TRAIN THE ARTIFICIAL
25 INTELLIGENCE OR ANY OTHER ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE; AND

26 (III) PROVIDES WRITTEN AND VERBAL NOTICE TO EACH
27 PATIENT:

28 1. THAT THE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER
29 MAY USE A SYSTEM THAT EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE TO PERFORM
30 ADMINISTRATIVE SUPPORT TASKS; AND

1 2. OF THE POTENTIAL RISKS ASSOCIATED WITH THE USE
2 OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE, INCLUDING THE POTENTIAL FOR A BREACH OF
3 CONFIDENTIALITY.

4 (2) A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER THAT USES A SYSTEM
5 THAT EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE FOR ADMINISTRATIVE SUPPORT TASKS
6 SHALL INDEPENDENTLY REVIEW THE ACCURACY OF ANY REPORT, DATA, OR ANY
7 OTHER INFORMATION THE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE COMPILES, SUMMARIZES,
8 ANALYZES, OR GENERATES.

9 (D) A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE PROVIDER SHALL OBTAIN WRITTEN
10 INFORMED CONSENT FROM EACH PATIENT IF THE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE
11 PROVIDER USES A SYSTEM THAT EMPLOYS ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE THAT COULD
12 MATERIALLY AFFECT CLINICAL DECISION–MAKING SERVICES OR PATIENT–FACING
13 SERVICES.

14 (E) A PERSON WHO VIOLATES THIS SECTION IS SUBJECT TO DISCIPLINARY
15 ACTION BY THE APPROPRIATE HEALTH OCCUPATION BOARD.

16 (F) THIS SECTION DOES NOT PROHIBIT A BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE
17 PROVIDER FROM DISSEMINATING SELF–HELP MATERIALS OR EDUCATIONAL
18 MATERIALS THAT DO NOT CLAIM TO PROVIDE BEHAVIORAL HEALTH CARE.

19 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect
20 October 1, 2026.
