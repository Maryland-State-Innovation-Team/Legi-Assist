A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Cooperative Housing Corporations and Condominiums – Emergency Use of
3 Reserve Funds

4 FOR the purpose of providing that reserve funds of a cooperative housing corporation or
5 condominium may be used for purposes other than those specified in the funding
6 plan in an emergency and under certain circumstances; and generally relating to
7 cooperative housing corporations and condominiums.

8 BY repealing and reenacting, with amendments,

16 Annotated Code of Maryland
17 (2023 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

18 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
19 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

20 Article – Corporations and Associations

21 5–6B–26.1.

22 (g) (1) The governing body of a cooperative housing corporation shall, in
23 consultation with a person identified under subsection (d)(1) of this section, develop a
24 funding plan to determine how to fund the reserves necessary under this section.

1 (2) In developing the funding plan under this subsection, the governing
2 body shall select one of the following methods to achieve the reserve funding under this
3 section:

4 (i) The component method;

5 (ii) The cash flow method;

6 (iii) The baseline funding method;

7 (iv) The threshold cash flow method; or

8 (v) Any other funding method consistent with generally accepted
9 accounting principles.

10 (3) A funding plan developed under this subsection shall prioritize
11 adequate amounts for repair and replacement of common elements of the cooperative
12 housing corporation that are necessary for:

13 (i) The health, safety, and well–being of the occupants;

14 (ii) Ensuring structural integrity, such as roofing replacements and
15 maintaining structural systems;

16 (iii) Essential functioning, such as plumbing, sewer, heating, cooling,
17 and electrical infrastructure; and

18 (iv) Any other essential or critical purpose, as determined by the
19 governing body.

20 (4) Reserves may be used for purposes other than those specified in the
21 funding plan IN AN EMERGENCY if [the]:

22 (I) THE funds are repaid to the reserve fund within 5 years after
23 their use; AND

24 (II) THE USE OF THE FUNDS IS APPROVED BY AN AFFIRMATIVE
25 VOTE OF AT LEAST TWO–THIRDS OF THE MEMBERS IN GOOD STANDING IN THE
26 COOPERATIVE HOUSING CORPORATION.

27 (5) A governing body shall review progress toward compliance with the
28 funding plan developed under this subsection at each annual meeting of the governing
29 body.
