A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Education – Public School Students – Recognition of External Diagnosis of
3 Autism

4 FOR the purpose of requiring a school to recognize a student’s external diagnosis of autism
5 from certain professionals until the school completes an initial evaluation; requiring
6 a school to initiate an evaluation of a student within a certain time frame and under
7 certain circumstances; requiring a school to provide certain supports to a student
8 during a certain evaluation and until a certain determination is made; and generally
9 relating to the recognition of an external diagnosis of autism in public school
10 students.

1 (a) The State Board shall adopt, as regulations, standards for the identification,
2 evaluation, educational placement, and the provision of a free appropriate public education
3 of each child in this State who has begun the school year under the age of 21 and is found
4 to need special education and related services, whether or not the child is receiving
5 nonduplicative services from another governmental agency.

6 (E) (1) A SCHOOL SHALL RECOGNIZE A STUDENT’S EXTERNAL DIAGNOSIS
7 OF AUTISM FROM A MEDICAL, MENTAL HEALTH, OR EDUCATIONAL PROFESSIONAL
8 AS A DIAGNOSIS UNTIL AN INITIAL EVALUATION OF THE STUDENT IS COMPLETED BY
9 THE SCHOOL.

10 (2) FOR THE PURPOSES OF THIS SUBSECTION, A MEDICAL, MENTAL
11 HEALTH, OR EDUCATIONAL PROFESSIONAL INCLUDES:

12 (I) A LICENSED DEVELOPMENTAL PEDIATRICIAN;

13 (II) A LICENSED CLINICAL PSYCHOLOGIST;

14 (III) A LICENSED PSYCHIATRIST;

15 (IV) A LICENSED CLINICAL SOCIAL WORKER WITH AUTISM
16 DIAGNOSIS TRAINING;

17 (V) A LICENSED SPEECH–LANGUAGE PATHOLOGIST WORKING
18 AS PART OF A MULTIDISCIPLINARY DIAGNOSTIC TEAM; AND

19 (VI) A LICENSED OCCUPATIONAL THERAPIST WORKING AS PART
20 OF A MULTIDISCIPLINARY DIAGNOSTIC TEAM.

21 (3) A SCHOOL SHALL INITIATE A SCHOOL–BASED EVALUATION OF A
22 STUDENT WITHIN 30 DAYS AFTER RECEIVING:

23 (I) AN EXTERNAL DIAGNOSIS OF AUTISM; AND

24 (II) WRITTEN CONSENT FROM THE STUDENT’S PARENT.

25 (4) (I) DURING THE EVALUATION PERIOD, A SCHOOL SHALL
26 PROVIDE TO A STUDENT WITH AN EXTERNAL AUTISM DIAGNOSIS THE SUPPORTS
27 RECOMMENDED IN THE DIAGNOSIS.

28 (II) A SCHOOL MAY PROVIDE ALTERNATIVE SUPPORTS TO A
29 STUDENT WHEN A SPECIFIC RECOMMENDATION:

30 1. CONFLICTS WITH FEDERAL OR STATE LAW; OR
