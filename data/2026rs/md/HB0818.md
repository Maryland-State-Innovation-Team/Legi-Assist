A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Higher Education – Foster Care Recipients and Homeless Youth – Tuition
3 Exemption and Associated Benefits

4 FOR the purpose of requiring public institutions of higher education to provide free of
5 charge room, board, and required books, course materials, supplies, and equipment
6 to certain foster care recipients and homeless youth who qualify for a certain tuition
7 exemption and enroll as students at the institutions; and generally relating to tuition
8 exemptions and student benefits for foster care recipients and homeless youth.

10 (ii) “Tuition” includes charges for registration and all fees required
11 as a condition of enrollment in credit–bearing and noncredit courses offered during the fall,

13 [(6)] (7) “Vocational certificate” means a certificate or license awarded by
14 a public institution of higher education on completion of a course of study that prepares an

16 (b) (1) A foster care recipient or homeless youth is exempt from paying any
17 tuition at a public institution of higher education, regardless of that foster care recipient’s
18 or homeless youth’s receipt of any scholarship or grant if:

19 (i) The foster care recipient or homeless youth is enrolled at the
20 institution on or before the date that the foster care recipient or homeless youth reaches
21 the age of 25 years;

22 (ii) The foster care recipient or homeless youth is enrolled as a
23 candidate for a vocational certificate, an associate’s degree, or a bachelor’s degree; and

24 (iii) The foster care recipient or homeless youth has filed for federal
25 and State financial aid.

26 (2) If a foster care recipient or homeless youth receives a scholarship or
27 grant for postsecondary study and is enrolled before the recipient’s 25th birthday as a
28 candidate for a vocational certificate, an associate’s degree, or bachelor’s degree at a public
29 institution of higher education, the scholarship or grant may not be applied to the tuition
30 for the foster care recipient or homeless youth.

31 (3) A foster care recipient or homeless youth who is exempt from tuition
32 under this section continues to be exempt until the earlier of:

33 (i) 10 years after first enrolling as a candidate for an associate’s
34 degree or a bachelor’s degree at a public institution of higher education in the State; or
