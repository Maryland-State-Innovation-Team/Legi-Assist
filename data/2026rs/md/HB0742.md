1 (3) “Family child care” means the care given to a child under the age of 13
2 years or to a developmentally disabled person under the age of 21 years that is in place of
3 parental care for less than 24 hours a day, in a residence other than the child’s residence,
4 for which the family child care provider is paid.

5 (4) “Family child care provider” means an individual who cares for children
6 in a registered family child care home or a registered large family child care home.

7 (5) “Local program” means a local jurisdiction or child care licensing region
8 that participates in the Program.

9 (6) “Program” means the Growing Family Child Care Opportunities
10 Program.

11 (7) “Start–up assistance” means the funding for starting a family child care
12 program including the cost of materials, curriculum, and renovations.

13 (b) (1) There is a Growing Family Child Care Opportunities Program in the
14 Department.

15 (2) The purpose of the Program is to provide grants to establish and
16 support local programs in the State.

17 (3) The Department shall partner with the Maryland Child Care Resource
18 Network to administer the Program.

19 (c) (1) Except as provided in subsection (g) of this section, on or before
20 September 1, 2022, the Department shall award at least three grants, each of which does
21 not exceed $150,000, in accordance with paragraph (2) of this subsection to establish and
22 support family child care providers in the State.

23 (2) (i) To be eligible for a grant under this section, a county governing
24 body or multiple county governing bodies shall sign a memorandum of understanding with
25 a child care resource center that serves the child care licensing region that contains one of
26 the counties.

27 (ii) The county or counties and the child care resource center jointly
28 shall apply for the grant and administer the local program.

29 (e) (1) Subject to paragraph (2) of this subsection, for each of fiscal years 2023,
30 2024, [and] 2026, AND 2028 THROUGH 2030, the Governor shall include in the annual
31 budget bill an appropriation of $450,000 to the Program.

32 (2) To the extent authorized under federal law, the Governor shall use
33 federal funds to satisfy the requirement of paragraph (1) of this subsection.

34 (g) (1) The Department may seek funding to start the Program.
