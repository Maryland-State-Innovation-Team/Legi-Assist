A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Walter Sondheim Jr. Public Service Internship Scholarship Program – Funding

3 FOR the purpose of specifying the intern fellowships that exist in the Walter Sondheim Jr.
4 Public Service Internship Scholarship Program; requiring the Governor to include in
5 the annual budget bill an appropriation for the Program that is at least a certain
6 percentage of the appropriation provided in a prior fiscal year; and generally relating
7 to the Walter Sondheim Jr. Public Service Internship Scholarship Program.

16 Service and Civic Innovation, launching the service year option for high school graduates,
17 and expanding on the promise of Maryland Corps; and

18 WHEREAS, Governor Wes Moore has continued to build on his work by allocating
19 over $50 million in both the fiscal year 2025 and fiscal year 2026 budget bills to provide
20 public service in the State, including securing federal funding; and

2 18–1702.

3 (a) (1) There is a scholarship program known as the Walter Sondheim Jr.
4 Public Service Internship Scholarship Program in the State.

5 (2) THE PROGRAM CONSISTS OF AT LEAST THE FOLLOWING PUBLIC
6 SERVICE INTERN FELLOWSHIPS:

7 (I) THE GOVERNOR’S SUMMER INTERNSHIP PROGRAM;

8 (II) THE SONDHEIM MARYLAND NONPROFIT LEADERSHIP
9 INTERNSHIP PROGRAM; AND

10 (III) THE SONDHEIM PUBLIC SERVICE LAW FELLOWS
11 PROGRAM.

12 (b) The purpose of the Program is to assist college and graduate students to
13 explore public service career opportunities through internships.

14 (c) The Shriver Center shall administer the Program.

15 (d) An institution of higher education in the State may nominate eligible students
16 to participate in the Program on or before:

17 (1) January 1 of each year for summer and fall internships; and

18 (2) October 1 of each year for spring internships.

19 (e) Priority for participation in the Program shall be given to an eligible student
20 who:

21 (1) Is a resident of the State;

22 (2) Has demonstrated an interest in a career in public service; and

23 (3) Assists in providing:

24 (i) Legal services:

25 1. To low–income residents in the State who cannot afford
26 legal services; or

27 2. In a public service position;
