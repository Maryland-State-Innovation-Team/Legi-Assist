A BILL ENTITLED

1 AN ACT concerning

2 Baltimore City – Alcoholic Beverages – 46th Alcoholic Beverages District

3 FOR the purpose of authorizing the Board of License Commissioners for Baltimore City to
4 authorize the transfer of a Class B–D–7 license from an area in the 40th alcoholic
5 beverages district into the 46th alcoholic beverages district; authorizing a license
6 holder in a certain area in the 46th alcoholic beverages district to keep and allow
7 individuals to consume alcohol donated by a manufacturer or wholesaler under
8 certain circumstances; extending for a certain amount of time the expiration of an
9 alcoholic beverage license issued for a certain area for certain transfer and renewal
10 purposes; and generally relating to alcoholic beverages in Baltimore City.

11 BY repealing and reenacting, without amendments,
12 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis
13 Section 12–102 and 12–1604(a)
14 Annotated Code of Maryland
15 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

18 Section 12–1604(k)
19 Annotated Code of Maryland
20 (2024 Replacement Volume and 2025 Supplement)

21 SECTION 1. BE IT ENACTED BY THE GENERAL ASSEMBLY OF MARYLAND,
22 That the Laws of Maryland read as follows:

23 Article – Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis

24 12–102.

25 This title applies only in Baltimore City.

9 SECTION 2. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, notwithstanding §§ 6–308
10 and 6–311 of the Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis Article, a licensed establishment on
11 the south side of the unit block of West Pratt Street may keep and allow individuals to
12 consume on the licensed premises alcoholic beverages donated by a manufacturer or
13 wholesaler if:

14 (1) the alcoholic beverages are donated and served at an event for which a
15 nonprofit entity has rented the licensed premises;

16 (2) the licensee has provided to the Board of License Commissioners for
17 Baltimore City evidence of the nonprofit status of the entity renting the licensed premises;

18 (3) the licensee serves the alcohol donated for the event;

19 (4) any related taxes have been remitted; and

20 (5) the licensee only keeps and serves alcoholic beverages in conjunction
21 with a single rental contract.

22 SECTION 3. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That, notwithstanding § 12–2202
23 of the Alcoholic Beverages and Cannabis Article, a Class B–D–7 beer, wine, and liquor
24 license issued for a premises in the 1600 block of Eastern Avenue shall be considered
25 unexpired until the end of July 1, 2027, for the purpose of being renewed to the current
26 license year and transferred to a new owner at a premises in the 700 block of Aliceanna
27 Street.

28 SECTION 4. AND BE IT FURTHER ENACTED, That this Act shall take effect June
